About The Workshop

Battery management systems (BMS) are essential to the efficient, safe, and reliable operation of battery energy storage systems, particularly in applications such as electric vehicles, renewable energy systems, and consumer electronics. As battery technologies evolve, so do the challenges associated with managing their performance, longevity, and safety. Advanced BMS is a topic that has garnered substantial attention, especially within the systems and control research community, over the past decade. The concerted efforts of researchers have yielded remarkable progress in harnessing control theory to enable complex, high-performance battery systems. Furthermore, Machine Learning (ML) techniques are emerging as powerful tools to complement traditional control methods, and the interplay between control theory and ML holds immense promise for revolutionizing BMS.

In response to these advancements, we are excited to organize a full-day workshop that will bring together leading researchers in this dynamic field. The primary goals of the workshop are to facilitate the exchange of cutting-edge developments and to provide insights into future innovations. In addition, it aims to create an engaging environment for open discussions among participants, with the expectation that these interactions will spark innovative ideas and inspire new directions in advanced BMS research.

The workshop will feature eight to ten speakers, each with extensive expertise in this domain and representing diverse backgrounds across academia and industry. Their presentations will cover key aspects of advanced battery management, focusing on the integration of electrochemical modeling, control theory, and ML, as well as the interplay between these approaches. This workshop aligns seamlessly with the mission of the American Control Conference to showcase cutting-edge research in control systems and their real-world applications. The American Control Conference also serves as an ideal platform for hosting this impactful event.

In addition, we are highly motivated to promote diversity, equity, and inclusion in all aspects of this workshop. Our efforts include ensuring gender diversity by inviting both males and females and promoting background diversity by featuring speakers from academia and industry, gathering from various locations around the world (i.e., spatial diversity). This is already a testament to our dedication to facilitating a diverse workshop. In addition, we will encourage participation from individuals of diverse backgrounds, including but not limited to gender, race, ethnicity, religion, and sexual orientation. We are committed to creating an inclusive and welcoming environment that values and respects the contributions and perspectives of all participants.

We look forward to this exciting workshop, where the exchange of knowledge, ideas, and experiences will drive us closer to realizing the full potential of control and ML techniques for advanced BMS.
